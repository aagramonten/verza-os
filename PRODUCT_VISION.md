# Product Vision

Verza OS is the operating system for landscape companies.

It is not a chatbot. It is not just a CRM. It is not a collection of disconnected tools. Verza OS is the business command center that helps a landscape company sell better work, deliver that work profitably, and build durable customer relationships.

The first tenant is Verza Garden in Puerto Rico. The product must serve Verza Garden immediately while being designed from day one as a multi-tenant SaaS platform for thousands of landscape companies.

## Mission

Help landscape companies become more profitable.

Profitability is the product's north star. Every workflow should help the company win more valuable jobs, quote faster, reduce administrative drag, track real costs, automate follow-up, retain customers, and understand which marketing channels produce profitable work.

## Core Goals

Verza OS exists to:

- Increase sales.
- Increase average project value.
- Reduce administrative work.
- Reduce quoting time.
- Track profitability.
- Automate follow-ups.
- Automate maintenance.
- Track marketing ROI.
- Track customer lifetime value.

These goals should guide product decisions. A feature that looks useful but does not support revenue, profit, operational clarity, customer retention, or scalable administration should be questioned before implementation.

## What Verza OS Is

Verza OS is a landscape business platform with connected modules for:

- Customer and lead management.
- AI-assisted intake and qualification.
- Site visit scheduling.
- Estimates and approved quotations.
- Projects and work execution.
- Invoices and payments.
- Recurring maintenance.
- Expenses and materials.
- Employees and labor tracking.
- Marketing attribution.
- Profitability analytics.
- Multi-tenant SaaS operations.

The product should feel like a practical operating system for a working landscape company: fast, clear, trustworthy, and focused on decisions that affect revenue and profit.

## What Verza OS Is Not

Verza OS is not a generic chatbot. Vera is only one interface into a larger business system.

Verza OS is not just a CRM. Leads and contacts matter, but the product must follow the full business loop from marketing source to lead, site visit, estimate, quote, project, invoice, maintenance, repeat work, and profit analysis.

Verza OS is not an autonomous quoting machine. Official quotes require human approval. The system can calculate preliminary estimates and prepare drafts, but the business remains in control.

Verza OS is not a support desk. Customer support may become part of the platform later, but the primary product motion is sales, operations, and profitability for landscape companies.

## The Business Loop

The most important product loop is:

1. A potential customer arrives from a marketing source.
2. Vera or a form captures the project need.
3. The system qualifies the opportunity and encourages photos, measurements, and a site visit.
4. A lead is created with source, service, budget signal, urgency, location, customer confidence, and buying signals.
5. The company schedules a site visit.
6. A preliminary estimate becomes a human-reviewed quote.
7. An approved quote becomes a project.
8. The project tracks revenue, labor, materials, equipment, fuel, advertising cost, and other expenses.
9. The system calculates profit and margin.
10. The customer is followed up for maintenance, repeat work, referrals, and future projects.

Every module should strengthen this loop.

## Vera

Vera is the conversational front door for Verza OS.

Vera is:

- A landscape consultant.
- A sales advisor.
- A project intake specialist.

Vera helps customers describe what they want, understand what information matters, feel less anxious, and take the next step. Vera should speak like an experienced landscaping consultant who knows the trade, explains why questions matter, and keeps the conversation moving without overwhelming the customer.

Vera is never:

- A customer support chatbot.
- A secretary.
- A quote approver.

Vera does not approve official quotes, invent prices, bypass business rules, or overwrite confirmed customer data. Vera can assist with intake, qualification, recommendations, preliminary estimates produced by the server, and scheduling momentum.

## Vera's Sales Purpose

Every Vera conversation must move toward scheduling a site visit.

The site visit is the conversion anchor because landscape work is visual, physical, and trust based. Photos and measurements help, but the visit is where the company confirms scope, builds confidence, and moves the customer toward a real quote.

Vera should:

- Ask one relevant question at a time.
- Encourage photos because they improve estimate quality.
- Encourage measurements when useful, while reassuring customers who do not know them.
- Detect hesitation and reduce friction.
- Explain the value of a site visit.
- Offer concrete next steps.
- Hand off to a human when trust, urgency, or complexity requires it.

## Product Principles

The server decides. The application must be the source of truth for state, permissions, pricing, scoring, and business rules.

Profit must be visible. A project is not complete as a business record until the company can understand revenue, costs, profit, margin, and hours worked.

Follow-up should be automatic. Landscape companies lose money when leads, quotes, maintenance visits, and past customers are forgotten.

Marketing must connect to profit. The product should not only count leads by source; it should connect campaigns and channels to revenue, profit, lifetime value, and close rate.

Maintenance is a growth engine. Recurring maintenance should be treated as a first-class business line connected to projects, customers, visits, labor, materials, and renewals.

Human approval matters. The system should accelerate work without removing accountability from business-critical decisions like quote approval, customer confirmation, pricing changes, payments, and user permissions.

Multi-tenancy is foundational. Even when serving one company, the product must be designed as SaaS from the beginning.
